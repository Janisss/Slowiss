To work as a nurse in Austria you need a state recognition that acknowledges you as a registered general nurse and entitles you to practice the profession.

Professional qualification recognition

Naczelna Izba Pielęgniarek i Położnych, validates equality of medical professions to applicants that achieved their qualification in Polish Republic and want to practice their profession in other member state.

In case that your training programme started after 1 May 2004:

You do not need Confirmation of qualification recognition, because of the automatic recognition based on Directive 2005/36/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council on the recognition of professional qualifications.

In case that your training programme started before 1 May 2004:

It is required to obtain a certificate from the Naczelnej Izby Pielęgniarek i Położnych to confirm that you have effectively and lawfully been engaged in nursing practice for at least three consecutive years during the last five years. The certificate must state it was awarded to you in accordance with article 33(a) of Directive 2005/36/EC.

Nostrification

To perform any regulated profession in the Austria the nostrification of diploma is inevitable, to determine the equality of two titles. The competent authority for the recognition of foreign qualifications is the Austrian National Authority for Qualifications.

Documents needed when submitting an application (translated into German)

- Application
- Curriculum vitae
- Austrian residence permit or Austrian postal address with an authorised recipient for formal delivery
- Formal qualification confirming officials recognised and successfully completed training
- Diploma supplement
- Evidence of authorisation to work as a general nurse
- Proof of professional experience – if possible
- Attestation
- Police clearance certificate from your origin country
- Medical certificate
- Wedding or divorce certificate
- Evidence of citizenship
