In the UK we distinguish domiciliary and live-in care.

Domiciliary care

A care giver is required to visit care taker in his/her own home and to help him/her with any daily activities person cannot safely manage on his/her own (for example: getting dressed, washing around the house, going to the toilet...). How often they visit and how long each visit lasts, depends upon how many one-to-one care minutes/hours care taker requires.

Requirements:

- Have a communicative level of English language

- Have a previous experience as a care giver or in a similar role is a plus

- Attend a training (usually lasts 2-5days)

- Have a clear DBS check

- Have a valid driving licence

Benefits of domiciliary care:

- Long term employment

- Excellent rates of pay - starting at 7.25 pounds/hour

- Training provided

- 28 days paid holiday

- Opportunity to work overtime

- National insurance contributions

- Mileage allowance

Live-in care

Care giver lives with the care taker in his/her own home, 7 days a week to assist him/her with daily routine. This could be help with house work, personal care, shopping or could just simply be for a bit of company. This helps client to be as independent as possible, relaxed in the knowledge that someone is on hand to help.

Documentation:

- Passport (recommended)

- European Health Insurance Card

- Driving Licence (if a driver)

- Birth Certificate

- Marriage Certificate

- Minimum of two references from previous employments

- Proof of address – for example utility bill – issued in the last 3 months)

- Healthcare Certificate (if you have)

Benefits of live-in care:

- Rotations possible

- Excellent rates of pay

- Training provided

- Accommodation and board in the family

First step for self-employment in Romania is to obtain a Certificate of Trade Authorisation:


Trade Licensing Office

Visit Trade Licensing Office appropriate to the residential address of the natural person.

Form

Complete a form for a natural person https://www.onrc.ro/index.php/ro/inmatriculari/persoane-fizice/persoane-fizice-autorizate-pfa.

Health insurance

Register in the system of mandatory health insurance.

Tax

Register with the tax administrator.

Administrative fee

Pay the administrative fee and attach the receipt to the completed form Administrative fees related to Trade Licensing.

Authorisation

The authorisation to carry on a notifiable trade shall become effective for the natural person registered in the Commercial Register as of the date of notification if the notice is without errors.

Before you start an employment you shall receive an Employment contract concluded under Labour Code Law No. 53/2003 (Legea nr. 53/2003) of Coll. as amended.


The advantages that you get:

Regular work.

Holiday entitlement.

Wage compensation uder certain conditions.

State social contributions entitlement.

Specified notice period depending on worked years (after completed probational period).

The advantages that you get:
